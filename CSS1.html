Some paragraphs

A paragraph (from Ancient Greek παράγραφος (parágraphos) 'to write beside') is a self-contained unit of discourse Ancient manuscripts also divided sentences into paragraphs with line breaks (newline) followed by an initial at the beginning of the next paragraph. An initial is an oversized capital letter, sometimes outdented beyond the margin of the text.

Though not required by the orthographic conventions of any language with a writing system, paragraphs are a conventional means of organizing extended segments of prose A second common modern English style is to use no indenting, but add vertical white space to create "block paragraphs."
